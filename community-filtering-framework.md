# Community Filtering Framework

A 5-layer participant selection system for high-accountability 
communities.

---

## Why Filtering Matters

Most communities fail not because of bad content but because of 
wrong people. This framework filters for commitment before 
anyone enters the room.

---

## The 5 Layers

### Layer 1: Intention Screen
Ask the applicant to state in writing why they want to join.
Reject vague answers. Accept only specific, personal reasons.

### Layer 2: Sacrifice Test
Require something that costs them before entry.
This can be time, money, or a completed task.
Free entry produces low commitment.

### Layer 3: Values Alignment Check
List your community's core values explicitly.
Ask applicants to rank them and explain one.
Misalignment here disqualifies regardless of other scores.

### Layer 4: Skin in the Game Verification
Ask what they are currently building or working on.
Communities of doers require active participants, not spectators.

### Layer 5: Accountability Readiness Assessment
Ask directly: Can you commit to showing up every week?
Can you receive hard feedback without shutting down?
A yes on paper is not enough. Look for evidence in their history.

---

## How to Score

Each layer is pass or fail.
A candidate must pass all 5 layers to enter.
No exceptions. No negotiation.

---

## Notes

The strictness of your filter determines the quality of your room.
A room full of the wrong people at high volume is worse than 
a small room of the right people.

Filter hard. Protect the culture.

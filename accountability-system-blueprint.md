# Accountability System Blueprint

A complete system for designing accountability structures 
inside communities and organizations.

---

## The Core Problem

People do not fail because of lack of information.
They fail because there is no consequence for inaction 
and no witness to their progress.

Accountability solves both.

---

## The 3 Pillars

### Pillar 1: Visibility
Every member's commitments must be visible to the group.
Private goals produce private failures.
Public commitments produce public effort.

Tools: shared trackers, weekly reports, open dashboards.

### Pillar 2: Consequence
Every broken commitment must carry a cost.
The cost can be financial, social, or reputational.
Without consequence, accountability is just encouragement.

Tools: penalty deposits, public reporting, streak systems.

### Pillar 3: Rhythm
Accountability must be scheduled, not spontaneous.
Weekly is the minimum cadence.
Daily is ideal for high-intensity programs.

Tools: standing calls, daily check-in threads, automated reminders.

---

## The Accountability Stack

Level 1: Self accountability (journal, personal tracker)
Level 2: Peer accountability (1-on-1 partner)
Level 3: Group accountability (cohort or team)
Level 4: Public accountability (audience or community)

Most programs stop at Level 1. 
The most powerful programs run all 4 simultaneously.

---

## Weekly Accountability Meeting Structure

1. Wins from last week (5 minutes)
2. Misses from last week, no excuses (5 minutes)
3. Commitments for this week, specific and measurable (5 minutes)
4. One ask from the group (5 minutes)

Total: 20 minutes maximum. Discipline the meeting.

---

## Red Flags That Your System Is Broken

- People stop showing up but stay in the group
- Wins are celebrated but misses go unaddressed
- Commitments are vague and unverifiable
- The facilitator carries more energy than the participants

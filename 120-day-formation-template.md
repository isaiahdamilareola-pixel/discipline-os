# 120-Day Formation Program Template

A structured framework for running deep transformation 
programs inside accountability communities.

---

## Program Philosophy

120 days is long enough to build real habits and short enough 
to maintain urgency. This is not a course. It is a formation track.

---

## The 4 Phases

### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1 to 30)
Goal: Establish identity, values, and baseline commitments.

- Week 1: Orientation and covenant signing
- Week 2: Personal audit (where are you actually right now?)
- Week 3: Vision articulation (where are you going?)
- Week 4: First accountability check and group calibration

### Phase 2: Construction (Days 31 to 60)
Goal: Build the core discipline habits.

- Assign each participant a weekly deliverable
- Weekly group calls for progress reporting
- Peer accountability pairing begins
- First elimination review at Day 45

### Phase 3: Pressure (Days 61 to 90)
Goal: Test what was built under real conditions.

- Increase deliverable difficulty
- Introduce external challenges or assignments
- Peer review replaces facilitator review
- Second elimination review at Day 75

### Phase 4: Completion (Days 91 to 120)
Goal: Consolidate gains and transition to next level.

- Final project or output submission
- Public presentation of results
- Graduation and recognition
- Invitation to next cohort or advanced track

---

## Weekly Rhythm

Monday: Weekly intention set
Wednesday: Mid-week check-in
Friday: Deliverable submission
Sunday: Reflection and reset

---

## Elimination Policy

Participants who miss 2 consecutive weeks without prior notice 
are removed from the program. No refunds. No exceptions.
This is not cruelty. It is protection of the cohort culture.

---

## Success Metrics

- Completion rate target: 60 percent minimum
- Weekly participation rate target: 80 percent minimum
- Deliverable submission rate target: 75 percent minimum
